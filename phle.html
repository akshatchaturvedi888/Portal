The Rise And Relevance Of Online Education

Since the early part of the century, online education has become a fundamental portion of most higher-level institutions, according to one study, with 65.5 percent of all principal scholastic officials recording that online education is crucial to the long-term plan (Lytle, 2011). Millions of students have enrolled, taken, and received degrees through online classes (Huffpost, 2013). Recent findings show a 3.9 percent increase in 2016-2017, and 29.7 percent of students take at least one online course according to the Digital Learning Compass report (Stone, 2017). One of the primary reasons we are seeing such an increase in online classes is because of the final acceptance of the quality and reputation they are finally gaining. The expanding prestige of the online specialist arrives at a period of solid enrolment in online education at the college level. The rising acceptance of online education is equalled by the growing acceptance of the online professional as a central member of University direction, guidance, and leadership.
